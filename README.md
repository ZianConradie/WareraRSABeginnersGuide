### Key Beginner Terms

**BTC** - In-game currency of WarEra
**Work** (lightning bolt symbol) - Allows you to earn a salary by working for a another company
**Self-work** (Lightbulb symbol) - work for a company you own
**Production Point** - Earned from work done in factories (by you or your employees), used to
produce goods (more explanation below)

### 1. Basic UI Breakdown

The **bottom-left timer** shows how long until your natural regeneration tick happens.
Each bar has numbers with upward arrows (↑) — these show how much that stat
regenerates every hour. (10% of total capacity regenerates per hour)
You can only interact with your bars at specific segmented intervals:
● **Hunger:** increments of 1
● **Energy, Intelligence:** increments of 10
● **Health** - Max is Increments 10, can be less due to armor (will explain later)


Clicking Options (highlighted in green) allows for different map modes to be used

**Fast Leveling at Low Levels**
Levels 1–10 pass very quickly because XP requirements are low. During this phase, your
goal is to build your economy, not your combat power.
When you gain levels, you earn skill points. Early on, place them into:
● **Production** — increases what your company produces
● **Energy** — gives you more usable actions
● **Entrepreneurship** — Increases number of active companies
**Your Early Game Priority: Money**


The first few levels feel slow financially, but this is where you lay the foundation. Ignore
advanced combat for now—focus entirely on creating stable income. With money, everything
else (gear, crafting, scaling companies) becomes easier.

### Resources

**Resource Deposits (RD) - Provides Resource Bonus**
● **Raw materials** such as lead, iron, grain, limestone, etc.
**● Deposits reset daily and spawn randomly across map**
● Resource deposits can be depleted before the next day starts
● Factories benefit from a **+30% production bonus if built on a matching resource.**
● Due to randomness and depletion, RDs are unreliable for stable production.
○ I would not recommend moving your company to hunt down these deposits
**Strategic Resources (SR) - Provides Production Bonus
● Countries can choose one resource / item to specialise in
● Companies only receive the production to companies IN that Country &
Producing that Specific resource**
● Provide permanent nationwide production bonuses.
● +3% per unique SR and +1% for duplicates.
● These stack onto company output.

### How Production Points work:

Each time you work at a company (your own / someone else’s) you use 10
energy/entrepreneurship. This produces production points for the company.
As a player scales their Economic Skills, the production points gained for this 10 energy
increases. This is based on the player's Production Skill.
**Production Bonuses**
Additional % boosts to production are gained from
● Country Production Bonuses
● Resource Bonuses (based on region in which company is established) - ONLY
applies to **companies Producing Raw Materials
Production Formula**
Output = Production Skills (RNG +- 2) * Production Bonuses
**Companies**
● Built using concrete.
● Costs increase with every company you create (50 → 100 → 150 ...).
● Best locations: countries with large production bonuses.


○ Check via Ranks → Countries → Production Bonuses.
● You can move or change company type for 5 concrete.
**Company Upgrades:**
⭑⭑ **Focus on Automation early game
Automation/New Company Upgrade Order**
Company 1 → Automation lvl 2 → Company 2 → All to Automation lvl 3 → Company 3


**Taxes**
● When employees work for a company they pay a % tax to the country in which the
country is based
● This money is then used by countries for war or region upgrades
**Your Early Setup (suggestion)**
● Setup companies in the country with the highest production bonus
● No employees
● Iron & Steel / Limestone & Concrete companies
● Use Skill points on Entrepreneurship, Energy, Production and Company limit

### 5. Combat Guide

**Equipment Tiers**
● 6 tiers: common → mythic.
● New players: stick to green-tier or below — higher tiers are wasted early on.
**Equipment Types & Bonuses**
Armor is damaged by 1% for every hit. When the item breaks you receive scraps.


**Critical shots increase your damage dealt.
Everytime you hit, you have a chance to miss**. Your precision percentage, decreases the
chance of missing a shot. When you miss a shot your damage is decreased by a large
amount. You are also unable to land a critical shot when you miss. **It is very important to
increase your precision.
Boots & Gloves are the most important armor pieces**
● **Pants & Armour:** incoming damage reduction (instead of each hit costing 10 health,
they can cost 9, 8, 7 health etc)
● **Gloves:** precision (Prevents missing an attack)
● **Boots:** dodge chance (Dodge allows you to hit without using any health and armor
does not get damaged)
● **Helmet:** crit damage
● **Weapons:** base damage & crit %
**Simplified Combat Formula ( i just ignore this and shoot tbh)**
(Attack + Weapon) × RNG × Ammo × Buffs × Terrain Bonuses

### 7. How Battles Work

**Declaration of War**
● Only government congresses can start a war.
● Battles are fought region vs region.
● Each battle is a best of 3 rounds.
**Ground Points System**
● A round ends when a side reaches 300 ground points.
● Ground points are awarded every 2 minutes (a tick).
● The side with higher total damage in a tick gains the (ground points) GP.
● GP per tick increases as the round goes on.
○ 0-100 (total GPs) = 1 GP per Tick
○ 100-200 = 2 GP per Tick
○ 200 - 300 = 3 GP per Trick
**Battle Bonuses**
● **Bunker:** up to +25% dmg
● **National Orders:** up to +15%

```
● Resistance: up to +40%

```

### Combat Stat Progression Guide

Understanding how your stats should grow as you level is crucial for dealing consistent
damage and surviving fights. Here’s a clear progression path for new players:
**Key Stats to Focus On**
● Attack — determines base damage.
● Precision — increases clean hit chance (very important early!).
● Crit Chance — chance to crit.
● Crit Damage — how strong your crits are.
● Armor & Dodge — reduces incoming damage / chance to avoid hits.
● HP & Food — how much you can heal and survive.

### Recommended Stat Targets by Level Range


**Levels 1–5: Fire Ant Phase (Prioritise Precision & Crit)**
Early game you don’t have many skill points, so damage isn’t huge yet. You want to land hits
consistently.
● Precision: ~65%
● Crit: 20–25%
● Attack: 140–
● Crit Damage: 140–
● Armor/Dodge: 8–12 (not a priority yet)
● HP: 60–
● Hunger: 4–
Focus: Precision → Crit → Attack
Reason: Missing hits kills your damage more than anything at low levels.
**Levels 7–10: Scorpion Phase**
Now you start getting enough points to avoid dying instantl.
● Begin adding Armor and Dodge to survive more hits.
● Maintain good Precision so your accuracy stays reliable.
Focus: Precision → Armor/Dodge → Crit
**Levels 12–16: The Crit Phase**
You start landing more crits — damage spikes.
● Boost Crit Chance and Crit Damage.
● Keep Precision healthy (65–70%).
Focus: Crit → Crit Damage → Attack


**Levels 18–30: High-Damage Era**
Now you transition into strong builds.
● Increase Attack heavily.
● Maintain strong Crit and Precision.
● Add Armor/Dodge so you don’t get shredded.
Focus: Damage → Crit → “Everything”

### Level 20+ Ideal Stat Baseline

By around level 20, you should aim for roughly:
Attack: 180–
Precision: 70–75%
Crit Chance: 35%
Crit Damage: ~
Armor: 12–
Dodge: 12–
HP: 70–
Hunger: 5–
This makes you balanced enough to deal massive damage while surviving long enough to
output it.

### Summary Philosophy

```
● Very Low Level: Damage low, hits matter → maximize Precision.
● Low–Mid Level: Medium damage → build Precision + some survivability.
● Mid–High Level: High damage → maximize Crit + Crit Damage.
● High Level: Strong in all areas → boost everything, especially Attack + Crit.
```
